# Brand Name + `.com` Domain Research — AI Video Studio

**Date:** June 2026
**Goal:** Find a perfect, brandable product name with an **available `.com`** for a broad AI video/film studio brand.

## Product & naming strategy

The product **today** turns a song into a complete, story-driven AI-generated **music video**, fully automatically. The **roadmap** stretches the same engine to AI **news/notiziario clips**, **short films**, and eventually **animated cartoons** and **full films**. The name must therefore read as a broad **"AI video / film studio"** brand — it must NOT lock to "music."

Constraints applied while choosing:
- **Brandable, memorable, easy to spell and say.** Short preferred (ideally ≤ 10 chars, ≤ 3 syllables).
- **Bilingual safety (IT + EN):** the founder is Italian. Every finalist was checked to sound good and be pronounceable in both languages, with **no awkward/negative meaning** in either. (Several otherwise-available coinages were rejected on this rule — see "Rejected on meaning.")
- **Evokes** video / motion / story / studio / vision / light — without being generic or literal-categorical (avoiding `film-`, `music-`, `song-` lock-in).
- **Avoid** obvious trademark collisions and names that read as an existing company.

### Reality check on the `.com` market (important)
The short, clean, euphonic `.com` space is **effectively exhausted**. In this research, **every** clean real-word or pretty 2-syllable coinage that was tried (lumora, kineto, vistara, cinemora, luminel, framora, visora, orfeo, fabula, novella, evora, auria, lumera, lunaria, movira, luvira, veluna, vondela, cinello, cineboom, videora, filmio…) came back **TAKEN**. The available names below are genuinely distinctive coinages — the kind that survive precisely *because* they are novel. This is the trade-off: a free `.com` here means an invented word, not a dictionary word.

## Top 5 recommendations (available `.com`, conservative)

All five below have a **confirmed-available `.com`** (3/3 method agreement) and pass IT+EN pronounceability and meaning screens.

1. **Vondira** — `vondira.com` ✅
   The strongest all-rounder. A clean, premium, invented word with **no category lock**, so it carries a music-video product today and a full film/animation studio tomorrow without contradiction. Easy to say and spell in both Italian and English (von-DEE-ra), with a soft "vision/aria" resonance that hints at sight and sound without naming either. Distinctive enough to trademark and own. **Primary recommendation.**

2. **Orfevo** — `orfevo.com` ✅
   The most *meaningful* pick: it evokes **Orpheus**, the myth of a musician whose song moves through worlds — a near-perfect metaphor for "song → story → film." Lyrical and cultured in Italian, intriguing in English, and it scales naturally from music videos to short films, myth-driven animation, and features. Slight caution: "Orfeo" is a widely used cultural term, so run a trademark search in the relevant classes — but the coined `-evo` ending differentiates it well.

3. **Visonda** — `visonda.com` ✅
   A **vision-led** coinage that reads instantly as "sight/seeing" while staying brand-neutral across every format on the roadmap. Modern, soft, and easy in both languages (vee-SON-da). Pairs naturally with a visual/lens logo and a tagline about "seeing your idea." A safe, broad, ownable choice and a very close second to Vondira on brandability.

4. **Vyndira** — `vyndira.com` ✅
   Same euphonic family as Vondira but with a sharper, more contemporary mark thanks to the stylized "y." Excellent for a logo/wordmark and memorable once seen. The only minor cost is that "Vy-" may need to be spelled aloud once; otherwise it is fully pronounceable in IT and EN and completely category-neutral.

5. **Vondaria** — `vondaria.com` ✅
   The operatic, fuller-bodied cousin of Vondira. The buried "**aria**" gives a gentle nod to music (apt for launch) while the overall word stays broad enough for news, film, and animation. Slightly longer (4 syllables), so it ranks just behind the tighter options — but it is a beautiful, distinctive, available alternative, and worth securing alongside Vondira to protect the family.

> **Tactical note:** Vondira / Vondria / Vondaria / Vyndira are one phonetic family. Pick **Vondira** as the primary and consider defensively registering the closest 1–2 variants so a competitor can't sit next door.

## How availability was checked (methodology)

Each name's `.com` was tested with **three independent methods**, and a name was only called **AVAILABLE** when **all three agreed** (conservative; conflicts were marked "uncertain — verify manually"):

1. **RDAP via rdap.org** (bootstrap aggregator), following redirects:
   `curl -s -L -o /dev/null -w "%{http_code}" https://rdap.org/domain/NAME.com`
   Calibrated against known domains: **`google.com → 200`** (registered) and a **random nonsense string → 404`** (free). So **404 ⇒ AVAILABLE, 200 ⇒ REGISTERED**. (Note: without `-L`, rdap.org returns a 302 redirect — the `-L` flag is required for a correct read; an early pass without it was discarded.)
2. **RDAP via Verisign** (the **authoritative** `.com` registry RDAP), also with `-L`:
   `curl -s -L -o /dev/null -w "%{http_code}" https://rdap.verisign.com/com/v1/domain/NAME.com` — same 404/200 semantics.
3. **`whois NAME.com`** (binary present at `/usr/bin/whois`): looked for **"No match for"** / "NOT FOUND" / "No Data Found" ⇒ FREE; presence of `Domain Name:` ⇒ REGISTERED.

`dig +short NAME.com` was used only as a weak early signal and was **not** relied upon for verdicts (parked/registered domains often have no A record, and DNS can lag).

**Caveat:** Domain status changes constantly. These results were captured in **June 2026**. Before purchasing, re-run the check (or check at a registrar) and **buy immediately** if still free — distinctive `.com`s like these can be registered by anyone at any time.

### ⚠️ Domain reality (re-confirmed, and a methodology correction)
The bare **cute-`.com` space is exhausted.** Every clean 4-letter and nearly every pretty 5-letter cute coinage tried came back **TAKEN**: muvo, vibo, movo, kivo, luvo, zumo, tuno, vuno, vido, nuvi, vimo, lumo, nivo, muvio, vibz, muvva, vyvio, mooza, viboo, muvoo, vooma, vunda, muvella, veluno, vunara, nuvora, vimora, muvelo, nuvelo, movelo, zumara, kimora … all registered.

**Methodology correction (important for future passes):** `rdap.org` was returning **false 404s** under load this session (it called muvella/veluno/vunara/nuvora "free" when they are in fact registered). All "free" verdicts below were therefore re-verified against **two authoritative methods — Verisign `.com` RDAP *and* `whois`** — and only called free when **both** agreed. Treat any single-source rdap.org "free" as *unknown* until cross-checked.

**Consequence:** for a cute short name, the realistic clean variant is almost never the bare `.com`. The two viable paths are **(a)** a slightly-novel spelling whose `.com` survives, or **(b)** a cute name on a sparser TLD — **`.video` is genuinely the most open here** (verified likely-free: `muvo.video`, `vibo.video`, `luvo.video`, `tuno.video`, `muvio.video`, `vibz.video`), with `.app`/`get-`/`try-` mostly taken too.
